# Channel Strategy — Faceless Music History YouTube

## Concept

Faceless documentary-style channel about music history, artists, and cultural moments.  
**Format**: AI/stock visuals + recorded voiceover narration + original Suno music. No face on camera, no real music tracks (copyright).  
**Competitive advantage**: Narrative quality and storytelling — most faceless music channels fail on this.

---

## Copyright-Safe Approach

**What's allowed:**
- Mentioning song/album titles and themes
- Discussing historical facts and events
- AI-generated visuals that evoke an era (no real photos of artists)
- Original Suno music composed in the style of an era (not "in the style of [artist]")
- Public domain sheet music and classical recordings

**What's blocked:**
- Original recordings (master tracks) → Content ID strike
- Real artist photos/footage → Getty, labels
- Song lyrics (protected separately from recordings)
- AI voice clones of real artists (likeness/rights issues)
- "Old = free" is a myth — archived footage is almost always owned

**Key rule**: Generate Suno music *in a style/era*, never name a real artist in the prompt.

---

## Content Formats (4 Recurring Series)

### 1. "Rise and Fall" (Flagship, 8–12 min)
Full arc: how they rose, what broke them, what remained. High watch time, highest monetization.  
Example: "They Recorded Their Divorce — And the World Bought It 40 Million Times"

### 2. "One Night That Changed Everything" (6–8 min)
Single pivotal moment — one concert, one session, one scandal. Tight, dense storytelling.

### 3. "The Curse of [X]" (7–10 min)
Pattern tragedies: cursed albums, cursed studios, Club 27. High CTR from mystery hook.

### 4. "What Really Happened to…" (6–9 min)
Disappeared/forgotten artists. Strong search intent + curiosity.

**Shorts Engine**: Every long video → 4–6 Shorts (one dramatic fact each, 45–60 sec).

---

## Thumbnail Rules

- One dominant subject, close crop
- 2–4 words max, high contrast
- Yellow or white text on darkened background
- Subject looking AT the camera → higher CTR
- NO red arrows, NO garish "MrBeast" style → audience is 35–55, they read it as cheap
- AI-stylized visuals, not real photos (copyright + aesthetic consistency)

## Visual Production Stack

| Element | Tool |
|---------|------|
| Hero images (stills) | Higgsfield (Soul Location model for cinematic scenes) |
| Image animation | Kling / Runway / Veo (image-to-video) |
| B-roll stock | Pexels, Pixabay, Storyblocks |
| Fake archive texture | Add film grain, scratches, vignette to AI stills in CapCut |
| Camera movement | Ken Burns (slow pan/zoom on stills) = documentary feel |
| Music | Suno (always Instrumental mode) |
| Voiceover | ElevenLabs or recorded human voice |
| Assembly | CapCut Desktop |

**AI image prompt style** for this channel: `cinematic, analog grain, moody, dark studio, 1970s aesthetic, no text, no logos, photorealistic, 16:9`

**Always include in negative prompt**: `text, words, letters, logos, watermarks, modern technology, color grading presets, CGI look`

## Audience Psychology

Two viewer motivations:
1. **"Make me smarter"** — curiosity, trivia, learning. Hook with the surprising fact.
2. **"Make me feel something"** — nostalgia, drama, emotion. Deliver with the narrative.

Best performing angle: **true-crime energy applied to music** — the shocking fact + the human cost.

## Episode Pipeline

1. **Topic selection**: Pick story with documented drama + search demand
2. **Script**: Hook (0–15s paradox) → Open loop → 3–5 "bits" → Climax → Soft CTA
3. **Visuals**: Generate Higgsfield stills per 5s timecode → animate key frames
4. **Audio**: Record VO → Generate Suno tracks (T1–T5 library) → Assemble in CapCut
5. **Assembly**: Images → VO → Music → Color grade → Ken Burns → Export
6. **Upload**: SEO title, chapters, first comment (key quote from video)
7. **Shorts**: Cut 4–6 shorts from the long video within 24h of upload
