## Description
It's 1977. A record shop owner is arrested — not for theft, not for fraud, but for a single word displayed in his window. The word was on an album cover. And that arrest was exactly what the band's manager had been praying for.

This is the story of how a group of art-school outsiders, a King's Road provocateur, and one deliberately offensive word turned a cheap publicity stunt into the most influential album in British music history. Every ban, every cancelled show, every outraged headline only made them bigger. Until the establishment put the word itself on trial — and lost.

This is how getting banned became the greatest marketing campaign rock has ever seen.
